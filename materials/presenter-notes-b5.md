# Presenter notes - Practitioner track session 5: Exemptions in practice (checker)

**Session goal (one line).** Teach practitioners the Eighth-Schedule exclusions - especially the ongoing-relationship one - and let them watch messages fall in and out of scope on the live checker.

## Run of show (45 min)

Practitioner rhythm: 3 welcome / 15 concepts / 22 build-along / 5 Q&A. The ongoing-relationship correction is this session's headline; the checker demo makes it visible.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame: the first question is always "is this even marketing?" Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-16 Part 1 - The Eighth-Schedule exclusions (7 min) plus Part 2 the live checker (8 min).** The exclusions: transactional/service, market research/survey, B2B, emergency, public-agency, personal/domestic, and ongoing relationship. Demo the .dncbox: watch a message fall out of scope as you change the purpose.
- **16-40 Build-along - The ongoing-relationship exclusion plus Sort the cases (24 min).** Deep-dive the ongoing-relationship exclusion, then practitioners sort exempt versus specified on the checker.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Preview b6 (access and correction).

## Preflight
- Open the b5 session page, scroll to the .dncbox, click a preset (for example "Rewards offer to a current cardholder" or "Delivery receipt SMS") and change a dropdown to confirm the ruling plus cited Eighth-Schedule provision render.
- Rehearse the ongoing-relationship correction so it lands cleanly - this is the session's reason to exist.
- Have two or three borderline "is it a survey or a sales pitch?" examples ready for the sort.
- Have the full exclusion list to hand so the case-sorting stays fast.

## Never-cut beats
- **Ongoing-relationship covers voice too.** Under the current Act the ongoing-relationship exclusion applies to all channels, voice included. The "text/fax only, not voice" version is the superseded pre-2019 framework - do not rely on it. It needs genuine continuity or regularity and an on-topic message.
- **B2B is broad.** A message to an organisation for a purpose of that receiving organisation is not a specified message, so the DNC duties do not apply.
- **Sole purpose is the test for surveys.** A genuine survey is exempt (a gift for completing it does not by itself make it marketing), but a "survey" used to disguise a sales pitch is a specified message.

## Cuts if running long
Shorten the case-sorting build-along to a handful of cases done together. Keep the ongoing-relationship deep-dive and the checker demo - the correction is the whole reason this session exists. Trim the public-agency and personal/domestic exclusions to a passing mention.

## Quiz answers
1. **C** - Under the current PDPA the ongoing-relationship exclusion applies to all channels, voice included. The "text/fax only, not voice" version is the superseded pre-2019 framework.
2. **A** - A message to an organisation for a purpose of that receiving organisation is covered by the B2B exclusion; it is not a specified message, so the DNC duties do not apply.
3. **B** - A "customer survey" whose real aim is to steer people toward buying is a specified message. A genuine survey is exempt and a completion gift alone does not make it marketing, but sole purpose is the test.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Doesn't ongoing-relationship exclude phone calls?"** No - that is the old rule. Today it covers voice, text and fax, with genuine continuity and an on-topic message.
- **"Is any B2B message automatically exempt?"** If it goes to an organisation for a purpose of that organisation, yes - it is not a specified message. A message to an individual's personal number for personal purposes is different.
- **"Can we call it a survey to dodge DNC?"** No. If the sole purpose is really to sell, it is a specified message regardless of the label.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines before acting. The single point most likely to be taught wrong is the **ongoing-relationship exclusion applying to voice** - foreground that the text/fax-only version is stale. Also keep the DNC register check at **21 days, not 30** if it comes up.
