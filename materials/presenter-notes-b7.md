# Presenter notes - Practitioner track session 7: Breach response

**Session goal (one line).** Give practitioners the breach playbook - the notifiable test, the assess-then-notify timelines, and how to contain, notify well, and learn.

## Run of show (45 min)

Practitioner rhythm: 3 welcome / 15 concepts / 22 build-along / 5 Q&A. The two clocks (assess, notify) are the spine - keep them clean in Part 1.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame: a breach is the obligation with a stopwatch - the process matters as much as the fix. Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-22 Part 1 - Assess, then notify (9 min plus discussion).** Notifiable if significant harm OR 500 or more individuals. Assess within 30 calendar days of credible grounds; notify the PDPC no later than 3 calendar days after determining it is notifiable; notify individuals at the same time or after.
- **22-40 Part 2 - Contain, notify well, learn (9 min) plus build.** Containment first, then a clear notification, then the post-incident learning loop. Build-along: run Marina's breach clock with real dates.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Preview b8 (building the compliance system).

## Preflight
- Open b7, hit "Expand all", and confirm the breach-clock timeline diagram renders and zooms.
- Have the three numbers exact: 30 days to assess, 3 days to notify the PDPC, individuals at the same time or after.

## Never-cut beats
- **Two different clocks.** Up to 30 calendar days to ASSESS whether it is notifiable; once you decide it is, no more than 3 calendar days to NOTIFY the PDPC. Do not merge them.
- **Either trigger is enough.** Significant harm OR 500 or more individuals - you do not need both.
- **The intermediary alerts its client, not the PDPC.** A data intermediary that detects a breach in data it processes for a client must alert that organisation without undue delay; the organisation then runs the assess-and-notify clock.

## Cuts if running long
Compress the "contain, notify well, learn" narrative in Part 2 to bullet points. Keep "run Marina's breach clock" with dates - internalising assess-then-notify is the point. Trim the notification-drafting tips before cutting the timeline work.

## Quiz answers
1. **C** - A breach is notifiable if it causes significant harm OR affects 500 or more individuals. Either limb alone triggers notification.
2. **A** - Once you determine a breach is notifiable, you notify the PDPC no later than 3 calendar days after that determination. (The up-to-30-days window is for assessing whether it is notifiable.)
3. **B** - A data intermediary must alert the organisation it processes for without undue delay; that organisation then assesses and handles the PDPC and individual notifications.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"3 days or 30 days?"** Up to 30 to assess, then no more than 3 to notify the PDPC once you have decided it is notifiable.
- **"Who tells the customers?"** The organisation, at the same time as or after the PDPC - never before.
- **"A vendor caused it - is it their problem?"** The intermediary must alert you without undue delay, but the organisation owns the assessment and the notifications.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines and get proper advice on any real breach. If DNC comes up, keep the two mistaught points straight: the register check is **21 days, not 30**, and the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers **voice too**. Within this session, the common error is conflating the **30-day assess** window with the **3-day notify** deadline.
