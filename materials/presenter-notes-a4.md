# Presenter notes - Leader track session 4: Consent vs DNC + exemptions

**Session goal (one line).** Give leaders the two things that decide most marketing questions - whether you have consent, and whether the message is exempt - so they can tell their team which sends are safe, which need a check, and which need consent.

## Run of show (45 min)

Leader rhythm: 3 welcome / 17 concepts / 20 exercises / 5 Q&A. The ongoing-relationship correction is the headline - rehearse it so it lands in one clean sentence.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame: not every message needs a register check, and not every message even falls under DNC. Two levers - consent and exemptions. Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-12 Part 1 - Consent: the shortcut and the standard (9 min).** Consent overrides listing (the shortcut). The standard: clear, unambiguous, a positive action, evidenced. Silence and pre-ticked boxes are not consent.
- **12-20 Part 2 - The exemptions, what is out of scope (8 min).** The Eighth-Schedule exclusions: transactional/service, market research, B2B, emergency, and the ongoing-relationship exclusion. Land the ongoing-relationship correction here.
- **20-32 Exercise 1 - Consent, check, or exempt? (12 min).** Sort scenarios into the three buckets.
- **32-40 Exercise 2 - Draft Marina's consent line (8 min).** Write a consent line that would actually pass - specific, positive action, evidenced.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Preview a5 (breach and enforcement).

## Never-cut beats
- **Ongoing-relationship covers voice too.** Under the current Act the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers all three channels, voice included. The "text and fax only, not voice" version is the superseded pre-2019 framework - do not teach it. It also needs genuine continuity or regularity and an on-topic message.
- **Consent overrides listing.** With clear consent on file you may message a listed number; the register check is only needed when you do not have consent.
- **Consent needs a positive action and evidence.** Silence, inaction, and pre-ticked boxes are not consent - the person has to do the agreeing, and you have to be able to show it.

## Cuts if running long
Compress Exercise 2 (drafting the consent line) into a "spot what's wrong with this line" verbal round. Keep Exercise 1 (consent/check/exempt sorting) - it is the decision leaders most need to make. Trim the exemption examples down to transactional, B2B, and ongoing relationship before cutting anything else.

## Quiz answers
1. **B** - You may still send to a listed number if you have clear consent. Consent overrides the listing; the register check is only needed when you lack consent.
2. **C** - Valid consent needs a positive action and must be evidenced. Silence, inaction and pre-ticked boxes do not count.
3. **A** - Under the current PDPA the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers all three channels including voice, needs continuity and regularity, and the message must be on-topic. The "text/fax only, not voice" version is superseded.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Doesn't the ongoing-relationship rule exclude phone calls?"** No - that is the old pre-2019 rule. Today it covers voice, text and fax, provided there is a genuine ongoing relationship and the message is on-topic.
- **"Is an opt-out (pre-ticked) box consent?"** No. Consent requires a positive action on a clear notice; failing to untick is not agreeing.
- **"If someone consented, do I still check the register?"** No - consent is an alternative lawful basis and overrides listing. But keep the consent evidence.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines before acting. The single point most likely to be taught wrong here is the **ongoing-relationship exclusion applying to voice** - foreground that the text/fax-only version is stale. Also keep the DNC register check at **21 days, not 30** if it comes up.
