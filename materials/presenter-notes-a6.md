# Presenter notes - Leader track session 6: Governance & accountability

**Session goal (one line).** Show leaders the machine that keeps the org compliant after the training glow fades - a named DPO, written policies, a complaints process, and a management programme that runs on a cadence.

## Run of show (45 min)

Leader rhythm: 3 welcome / 17 concepts / 20 exercises / 5 Q&A. This is the finale of the leader track - close by pointing to the practitioner track for the hands-on build.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame: the first five sessions were rules; this is the system that keeps you following them after the training glow fades. Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-12 Part 1 - The DPO and the accountability obligation (9 min).** Every org must designate a DPO - the role is a focal point, not a lightning rod; the org stays legally responsible. Accountability equals written policies, a complaints process, staff who know the rules, and information available on request.
- **12-20 Part 2 - The compliance programme (DPMP) and SME help (8 min).** A DPMP is a cycle, not a checklist; periodic review feeds the next round. Free PDPC/IMDA tools - Data Protection Essentials, the DP Starter Kit, the PDPA Assessment Tool - let SMEs run it without a big budget.
- **20-32 Exercise 1 - Score Marina's accountability artefacts (12 min).** Rate what Marina has versus what she still needs.
- **32-40 Exercise 2 - Assign an owner and a cadence (8 min).** Who owns each artefact, reviewed how often.
- **40-45 Q&A / close.** Point leaders to the practitioner track (b1-b8) for the hands-on version.

## Never-cut beats
- **A DPO is mandatory and does not transfer liability.** Designation is required; it makes the DPO a focal point, but the organisation stays legally responsible for compliance.
- **Accountability is artefacts you can show.** Written policies, a complaints process, trained staff, information available on request - things an auditor can see, not good intentions.
- **A DPMP is a rhythm, not a one-off.** The periodic self-assessment is what stops it quietly rotting as staff, systems and data flows change.

## Quiz answers
1. **B** - Every organisation must designate at least one DPO; the role is a focal point and does not remove the organisation's legal responsibility for compliance.
2. **C** - Accountability requires artefacts you can show: written policies, a complaints process, staff who know the rules, and information available on request.
3. **A** - A DPMP is best understood as a cycle, not a checklist; the periodic review feeds the next round, and free PDPC/IMDA tools help SMEs run it.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Can our DPO be an existing employee?"** Yes - the DPO can be internal and part-time, but the designation must exist and their business contact info be available.
- **"If we appoint a DPO, are we off the hook?"** No. The DPO is a focal point; the organisation remains legally responsible.
- **"We're an SME with no budget - where do we start?"** The free PDPC/IMDA tools: Data Protection Essentials, the DP Starter Kit, and the PDPA Assessment Tool on a recurring date.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines before acting, and note the DPMP programme framing here is partly the course's own synthesis. If DNC comes up, keep the two mistaught points straight: the register check is **21 days, not 30**, and the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers **voice too**.
