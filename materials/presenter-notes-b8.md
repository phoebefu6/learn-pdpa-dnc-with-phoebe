# Presenter notes - Practitioner track session 8: Building the compliance system

**Session goal (one line).** Pull every practitioner skill into one standing system - the DPMP artifacts - and send Marina out with a 90-day plan.

## Run of show (45 min)

Practitioner rhythm: 3 welcome / 15 concepts / 22 build-along / 5 close. This is the course finale - the 90-day plan is what graduates carry out the door.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame the finale: individual tasks become a system that runs on rhythm, not panic. Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-22 Part 1 - The accountability artifacts (9 min plus discussion).** The DPMP components: a designated DPO with published contact info, internal policies, an external Data Protection Notice, a data inventory, training, a breach plan, and periodic self-assessment.
- **22-40 Part 2 - Marina graduates (9 min) plus build.** Walk Marina's full journey from b1 to b8. Build-along: score Marina's DPMP and build the 90-day plan.
- **40-45 Close.** Point graduates to the free PDPC/IMDA tools and to the leader track (a1-a6) for the governance view.

## Never-cut beats
- **A DPO with available contact info is mandatory.** Appointing a DPO and making their business contact info available is a required element of accountability - the other "nice to have" items are not PDPA requirements.
- **Internal policies and the external notice are different animals.** Internal policies tell staff how to handle data; the external Data Protection Notice tells individuals what you collect, why, and how to reach you. A DPMP needs both.
- **A programme is a rhythm.** Periodic self-assessment - the PDPA Assessment Tool on a recurring date - is what stops the DPMP quietly rotting as staff, systems and data flows change.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"What is the one non-negotiable?"** A designated DPO with published business contact info. Everything else builds around it.
- **"Policy or notice - aren't they the same?"** No. Policies are internal (for staff); the Data Protection Notice is external (for individuals). You need both.
- **"How do we keep this alive after today?"** Put the PDPA Assessment Tool on a recurring calendar date and treat it as the heartbeat of the programme.
