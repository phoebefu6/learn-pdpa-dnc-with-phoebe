# Presenter notes - Leader track session 5: Breach & enforcement

**Session goal (one line).** Give leaders the breach-notification clock and the real cost of getting it wrong - fixed timelines and actual PDPC decisions - so a Friday-evening breach finds them already knowing what to do.

## Run of show (45 min)

Leader rhythm: 3 welcome / 17 concepts / 20 exercises / 5 Q&A. The breach-clock timeline is the anchor visual - present it at the projector and walk the arrows.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Two leadership jobs when something goes wrong: know the clock, and know the cost. Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-12 Part 1 - The breach clock (9 min).** Notifiable if significant harm OR 500 or more individuals. Assess within 30 calendar days of credible grounds; notify the PDPC no later than 3 calendar days after determining it is notifiable; notify individuals at the same time or after, never before.
- **12-20 Part 2 - What it costs, real cases (8 min).** MBS S$315k, Ezynetic S$17.5k, the bought-list S$16.8k. Penalty caps: a DP breach up to S$1M or 10% of SG turnover, whichever is higher.
- **20-32 Exercise 1 - Run Marina through the clock (12 min).** Take a Marina Retail scenario and walk assess-then-notify with concrete dates.
- **32-40 Exercise 2 - Rank the mitigating and aggravating factors (8 min).** What raises the number, what lowers it.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Preview a6 (governance).

## Preflight

## Never-cut beats
- **The 30 days is to ASSESS, the 3 days is to NOTIFY.** These are two different clocks. You have up to 30 calendar days to work out whether it is notifiable; once you decide it is, the PDPC must hear no later than 3 calendar days after that determination.
- **Either trigger is enough.** Significant harm OR 500 or more individuals - you do not need both.
- **Checking DNC helped Lee Chee Meng.** Even though the bought list was the real breach, the fact he had checked the register was mitigating and reduced the penalty. The PDPC rewards what you did right.

## Cuts if running long
Compress Exercise 2 (ranking factors) into a quick verbal list. Keep Exercise 1 (running the clock with dates) - internalising assess-then-notify is the point of the session. Trim the penalty-cap detail before cutting the timeline.

## Quiz answers
1. **C** - A breach is notifiable if there is significant harm OR a scale of 500 or more individuals. Either trigger alone is enough.
2. **A** - Once you determine a breach is notifiable, you notify the PDPC no later than 3 calendar days after that determination; individuals are notified at the same time or after, never before. (The 30 days is the assessment window.)
3. **B** - Checking DNC was a mitigating factor for Lee Chee Meng. His real breach was using a bought list with no consent or notification, but the register check counted in his favour and the penalty was reduced.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Do we have 3 days or 30 days to tell the PDPC?"** Up to 30 days to assess whether it is notifiable; then no more than 3 calendar days to notify once you have decided it is.
- **"Can we tell affected customers before the PDPC?"** No - notify individuals at the same time as or after the PDPC, not before.
- **"What if a vendor caused the breach?"** A data intermediary must notify the organisation it processes for without undue delay; that organisation then runs the assess-and-notify clock.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines and get proper advice on any real breach. Watch the two mistaught points: keep the DNC check at **21 days, not 30** if DNC arises, and remember the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers **voice too**. Within this session, the common error is conflating the **30-day assess** window with the **3-day notify** deadline.
