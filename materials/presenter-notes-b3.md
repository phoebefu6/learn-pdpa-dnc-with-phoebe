# Presenter notes - Practitioner track session 3: A compliant campaign (checker)

**Session goal (one line).** Walk practitioners through one marketing campaign in five ordered steps - scope, lawful basis, send, record - and screen a real send live with the checker.

## Run of show (45 min)

Practitioner rhythm: 3 welcome / 15 concepts / 22 build-along / 5 Q&A. This session has two live-checker touchpoints (Part 1b and the build-along) - protect both.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame: a campaign done in the right order almost cannot go wrong. Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-22 Part 1 - The five-step campaign flow (8 min) plus Part 1b the live checker (8 min).** Step 1 scope (is it a specified message?), step 2 lawful basis (check or consent), step 3 send, step 4 honour opt-outs, step 5 record. Then demo the .dncbox: screen Marina's send end to end.
- **22-40 Part 2 - Records that prove it (8 min) plus build-along.** The evidence trail that survives an audit; practitioners build Marina's campaign checklist.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Preview b4 (message-ID plus calling rules).

## Preflight
- Open the b3 session page, scroll to the .dncbox, click a preset and change a dropdown to confirm the ruling plus cited provision render.
- Rehearse "Screen Marina's send" so you can drive the checker through a full campaign scenario without fumbling.
- Have the five steps memorised in order - scope is always first.
- Have a skeleton campaign checklist ready to fill in during the build-along.

## Never-cut beats
- **Scope comes before lawful basis.** Step 1 is always "is this even a specified marketing message?" If it is out of scope, the DNC duty never applies - so check that before spending effort choosing a basis.
- **21 days can be outrun by a long campaign.** A "not listed" confirmation is valid 21 days; if sends continue past day 21, re-check first. Consent, by contrast, does not expire on a timer.
- **A mid-campaign withdrawal stops that send.** If a customer withdraws consent midway, stop sending to them, then re-establish a lawful basis (re-check the register within 21 days) before sending again.

## Cuts if running long
Compress "build Marina's campaign checklist" into a shared skeleton filled together. Keep the checker screening (Part 1b) and the five-step order - they are the spine of the session. Trim the records detail in Part 2 to naming the key evidence before cutting checker time.

## Quiz answers
1. **B** - Before choosing a lawful basis you scope the message (step 1). If it is not a specified marketing message it is out of scope and the DNC duty never applies.
2. **A** - A "not listed" confirmation is valid 21 days. A long campaign can outrun it - if sends continue past day 21, re-check first.
3. **C** - If a customer withdraws consent mid-campaign, stop the send, then re-establish a lawful basis by re-checking the register within 21 days before sending to them again.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Which comes first, checking DNC or picking a basis?"** Neither - scoping does. Confirm it is a specified message first; if not, DNC never applies.
- **"My campaign runs six weeks - is one check enough?"** No. The check is good for 21 days; re-check before sends that fall past that window.
- **"Someone opts out halfway - do I finish the batch?"** No. Stop sending to them immediately and re-establish a basis before any future send.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines before acting; note the campaign workflow here is partly the course's own synthesis. Foreground the two mistaught points: the register-check validity is **21 days, not 30**, and the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers **voice too** (b5). Do not quote a per-check dollar figure.
