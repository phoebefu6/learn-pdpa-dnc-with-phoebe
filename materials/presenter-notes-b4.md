# Presenter notes - Practitioner track session 4: Message-ID + calling rules

**Session goal (one line).** Teach the two identity duties that sit alongside the register check - sender identification (s.44) on every channel, and the calling-line duty (s.45) for voice and fax.

## Run of show (45 min)

Practitioner rhythm: 3 welcome / 15 concepts / 22 build-along / 5 Q&A. The 30-vs-21-day distinction is the trap this session must close.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame: passing the register check is not the finish line - the message must say who sent it and how to reach them. Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-22 Part 1 - Identify the sender, s.44 (8 min plus discussion).** Every specified message must clearly identify the sender and a way to contact them; that contact info must be reasonably likely to stay valid for at least 30 days. Applies to every channel.
- **22-40 Part 2 - Do not hide the number, s.45 (8 min) plus channels.** Voice calls and faxes must not conceal the sender's calling number. Build-along: audit a real Marina message against both duties.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Preview b5 (exemptions in practice).

## Preflight
- Open b4, hit "Expand all", and confirm the s.44 and s.45 diagrams render and zoom.
- Have a real marketing SMS and a call scenario ready to audit against s.44 and s.45.
- Rehearse the 30-day (s.44 contact info) versus 21-day (b1 register check) distinction so you never blur them.
- Know which duty maps to which channel before the build-along starts.

## Never-cut beats
- **The 30-day rule is s.44, NOT the register check.** s.44 requires the sender contact info to be reasonably likely to stay valid for at least 30 days. Do not conflate this with the 21-day register-check validity from b1 - separate rule, separate clock.
- **s.44 applies to every channel.** A checked-clear SMS with no sender name and no reply route still fails on s.44. Passing the register check is necessary but not sufficient.
- **s.45 is voice and fax only.** The calling-line duty (do not conceal the number) applies to voice calls and faxes, not to SMS.

## Cuts if running long
Compress the message audit build-along to one shared example done together. Keep the s.44-versus-s.45 channel distinction and the 30-versus-21-day clarification - those are the trap questions. Trim the channels detail in Part 2 before cutting either identity duty.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Is the 30-day rule the same as the DNC check window?"** No. 30 days is the s.44 sender-contact-info validity; 21 days is the register-check validity. Two separate clocks.
- **"Does the calling-line rule apply to text messages?"** No - s.45 is voice and fax only. SMS still needs s.44 sender ID and the register check.
- **"We passed the register check - are we done?"** Not necessarily. You still need a clear sender identity and contact route (s.44), and no concealed number on voice/fax (s.45).

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines before acting. The single easiest thing to teach wrong here is conflating the **s.44 30-day** contact-info validity with the **21-day** register-check window - keep them separate. If the ongoing-relationship exclusion arises, remember it covers **voice too** under the current Act.
