# Presenter notes - Practitioner track session 1: The DNC check (live checker)

**Session goal (one line).** Build the one habit that keeps a marketer compliant - check the register before you send - and let practitioners rule any scenario live with the compliance checker.

## Run of show (45 min)

Practitioner rhythm: 3 welcome / 15 concepts / 22 build-along / 5 Q&A. The checker is the heart of this session, so protect the build-along time.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame: this is the single habit that prevents most DNC breaches. Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-10 Part 1 - Three registers, one duty (7 min).** No Voice Call, No Text Message, No Fax Message; the number is checked against all three at once. The duty: hold a "not listed" confirmation obtained within 21 days before you send.
- **10-18 Part 2 - The live compliance checker (8 min).** Demo the checker (.dncbox). Click through the presets, showing how the ruling and the cited provision update as inputs change.
- **18-40 Build-along - Rule any scenario / Break your own campaign (22 min).** Practitioners drive the checker: rule scenarios, then try to "break" their own real campaign by feeding its parameters in and watching what fails.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Preview b2 (capturing consent).

## Preflight
- Open the b1 session page, scroll to the .dncbox, click a preset (for example "Promo to a LISTED number, no consent") and change a dropdown to confirm the ruling plus cited provision (s.43 / s.44 / s.45 / Eighth Schedule) render correctly.
- Confirm all six presets load: SMS promo opted-in customer, Cold call no check, Promo to a LISTED number no consent, Delivery receipt SMS, Rewards offer to a current cardholder, Checked-clear SMS no sender ID.
- Read the "honesty note - what this checker really is" callout so you can explain it is a teaching model of the Part 9 decision logic, not the live dnc.gov.sg portal.
- Have the six dropdown fields clear in your head: message purpose, channel, consent on file, DNC register check, sender identity (s.44), calling line identity (s.45).

## Never-cut beats
- **21 days, NOT 30.** A "not listed" confirmation is valid for 21 days. The 30 you may still see in old PDPC material is the pre-2021 rule - stale. Re-check within 21 days before every send.
- **Consent overrides listing.** Clear, specific consent lets you message even a listed number - that is why the checker passes "consent on file" even when the number is listed.
- **Passing the check is necessary but not sufficient.** A checked-clear number with no sender ID still fails on s.44. Show this live with the "Checked-clear SMS, no sender ID" preset.

## Cuts if running long
Shorten the "Break your own campaign" half of the build-along and keep "Rule any scenario." Trim the register-mechanics talk in Part 1 before cutting checker time - the checker is the point of this session.

## Quiz answers
1. **B** - A "not listed" confirmation is valid for 21 days. The 30-day figure in older material is stale; re-check within 21 days before every send.
2. **C** - With clear, specific consent you may send even to a number that is on the register. Consent is an alternative lawful basis that overrides listing.
3. **A** - A checked-clear number with no sender identity still fails: every specified message must identify the sender and how to contact them (s.44), a separate duty from the register check.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Is the checker the real DNC Registry?"** No - it is a teaching model of the Part 9 decision logic. The real check happens on the dnc.gov.sg portal.
- **"How long is my clear result good for?"** 21 days. A long campaign can outrun it - re-check if sends continue past day 21.
- **"If I have consent, do I still need to check?"** No - consent overrides listing. But keep the consent evidence, and note it does not remove the s.44 sender-ID duty.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines before acting. The two points most likely to be taught wrong: the register-check validity is **21 days, not 30** (the stale brochure figure), and the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers **voice too** under the current Act (detail in b5). Do not quote a per-check dollar figure - it lives inside the portal.
