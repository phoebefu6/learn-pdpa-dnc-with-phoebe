# Presenter notes - Leader track session 2: The ten obligations

**Session goal (one line).** Give leaders all ten data-protection obligations on one page as plain questions their team should be able to answer, and spotlight the three that cause the most trouble.

## Run of show (45 min)

Leader rhythm: 3 welcome / 17 concepts / 20 exercises / 5 Q&A. The ten-obligation diagram is the centrepiece of Part 1 - present it at the projector.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame the ten as duties, not legal text: each is a question ("did we get consent? do we protect it? do we still need it?"). Read the not-legal-advice banner once.
- **3-12 Part 1 - The ten, on one page (9 min).** Name all ten grouped: consent, purpose, notification, access/correction, accuracy, protection, retention, transfer, breach notification, accountability. Flag clearly that Data Portability was enacted but is not yet in force, so the current count is ten.
- **12-20 Part 2 - The three that cause the most trouble (8 min).** Spotlight the high-risk trio - consent, protection, accountability - and explain why these draw the most enforcement. Use a5's real cases as forward references.
- **20-32 Exercise 1 - Score all ten (12 min).** Red/amber/green each obligation for the org. Locate the reds; do not solve them yet - that is the rest of the course.
- **32-40 Exercise 2 - Name the first fix (8 min).** Pick the reddest obligation and define the single first action plus an owner.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Tie back to a1's exposure map and preview a3 (the DNC layer).

## Preflight
- Open a2, hit "Expand all", and confirm the ten-obligation diagram renders and zooms.
- Have the exact obligation list memorised in order - leaders will test you by naming one and asking "which section is that?"
- Rehearse the Data Portability line; keep the "enacted 2020, not yet in force" phrasing exact so you never imply eleven.
- Have the red/amber/green scorecard ready to project or print for Exercise 1.

## Never-cut beats
- **The count is ten, not eleven.** Data Portability was passed (Part VIB) but has not commenced - do not teach it as a live obligation. Mention it, then set it aside.
- **Accountability is a "before anything leaks" duty.** A named DPO and written, published policies are required whether or not you ever have an incident. You can be in breach with zero data lost.
- **Consent cannot be forced or trapped.** You may not require consent beyond what is reasonable to provide the product/service, and consent can be withdrawn any time on reasonable notice.

## Cuts if running long
Compress Exercise 2 into a verbal "name your one reddest obligation and who owns the fix." Keep Exercise 1 - the full red/amber/green scorecard is the deliverable leaders carry out of the room. Trim the anecdotes in Part 2 before cutting any of the ten names.

## Quiz answers
1. **B** - The current count is ten. Data Portability was enacted in 2020 but has not commenced, so it is excluded.
2. **C** - Accountability makes a named DPO mandatory and requires written, published policies; without them you are in breach before anything leaks.
3. **A** - Under the Consent obligation you may not force consent beyond what is reasonable to provide the product/service, and consent can be withdrawn at any time on reasonable notice.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Is Data Portability the eleventh obligation now?"** No. It was enacted but is not yet in force. Mention it, do not rely on it.
- **"Do we really need a DPO if we're tiny?"** Yes. Designation is mandatory for every organisation; the DPO can be an existing staff member, but the role must exist and their contact info be available.
- **"Which obligation gets orgs in trouble most?"** Protection (reasonable security) drives the big penalties; consent and accountability are the quiet, common failures.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC Advisory Guidelines before acting. The point most likely to be taught wrong here is the **count**: it is ten, and Data Portability is not yet in force. If DNC comes up, also keep the register check at **21 days, not 30** and remember the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers **voice too**.
