# Presenter notes - Leader track session 3: The DNC layer

**Session goal (one line).** Give leaders the shape of the Do Not Call layer - three registers, the check-before-send duty and its 21-day window, and the "is this even a marketing message?" gate.

## Run of show (45 min)

Leader rhythm: 3 welcome / 17 concepts / 20 exercises / 5 Q&A. This is the session where the 21-day correction has to land, so slow down in Part 1.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame: DNC sits on top of the PDPA and governs one thing precisely - marketing messages to Singapore phone numbers. Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-12 Part 1 - Three registers + the core duty (9 min).** No Voice Call, No Text Message, No Fax Message; numbers are checked against all three at once. The core duty: hold a "not listed" confirmation before you send, valid for a limited window. State clearly the window is 21 days.
- **12-20 Part 2 - What even counts as marketing (8 min).** The "specified message" test: a purpose to advertise or promote goods, services, land, or a business/investment opportunity. If it is not a specified message, DNC does not apply at all.
- **20-32 Exercise 1 - Sort the messages (12 min).** Give real message examples; leaders sort specified versus out-of-scope.
- **32-40 Exercise 2 - Prove the lawful basis (8 min).** For each in-scope message, name the basis: register check, consent, or exemption.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Preview a4 (consent versus DNC and the exemptions in depth).

## Preflight

- Have the three register names exact and the 21-day figure front of mind - this is the number the session exists to correct.
- Pre-sort two or three example messages yourself so Exercise 1 moves fast.
- Be ready to explain why the old 30-day figure is still floating around (superseded 2021).

## Never-cut beats
- **21 days, NOT 30.** A "not listed" confirmation is valid for 21 days since 1 Feb 2021. Older PDPC material still says 30 - it is stale. Say this out loud and name why the number people remember is wrong.
- **The first question is "is this even a specified message?"** If not - a receipt, a B2B note - the DNC registers do not apply at all. Do not burn effort on a register check for something out of scope.
- **Consent overrides listing.** Clear consent lets you message even a listed number - the register protects people who have not agreed to hear from you, and genuine consent means they have.

## Quiz answers

2. **A** - The first question is whether it is a specified (marketing) message at all. If not - a receipt, a B2B note - the DNC registers do not apply.
3. **B** - You may still text a listed number if you have clear consent. Consent overrides listing.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Isn't the register check good for 30 days?"** No - that is the pre-2021 rule still living in old brochures. It is 21 days now.
- **"Do I check one register or all three?"** All three at once; the number is screened against No Voice, No Text and No Fax together, and you honour whichever covers your channel.
- **"Is a survey or a receipt a marketing message?"** Usually not - if its sole purpose is transactional or genuine research it is out of scope. But a "survey" that is really a sales pitch is a specified message (see b5).

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines before acting. Foreground the two most-mistaught points: the register-check validity is **21 days, not 30** (the stale brochure figure), and although this session centres on the check, remember the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers **voice too** under the current Act (detail in a4/b5).
