# Presenter notes - Practitioner track session 2: Capturing consent

**Session goal (one line).** Teach practitioners to capture consent they can actually prove - valid consent, the deemed-consent routes, and how to handle withdrawal.

## Run of show (45 min)

Practitioner rhythm: 3 welcome / 15 concepts / 22 build-along / 5 Q&A. Part 1 and Part 2 each run to about the 22 and 40 mark; the "rewrite a bad consent line" build lives inside them.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame: consent is only useful if you can prove it. Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-22 Part 1 - What valid consent looks like (8 min live plus discussion).** s.14: a positive action on a clear notice; cannot be forced beyond what is reasonable to provide the product/service; cannot be obtained by false or misleading means. Pre-ticked boxes and silence are not consent.
- **22-40 Part 2 - Deemed consent plus withdrawal (8 min live plus build).** Deemed consent by conduct (s.15(1)), by contractual necessity (s.15(3)), by notification (s.15A - assessment plus opt-out window). Withdrawal (s.16): cannot be prohibited; inform of consequences, then cease use (about 10 business days), and record it.
- **Build-along - Rewrite a bad consent line (inside the blocks above).** Practitioners fix a failing consent line into a provable one.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Preview b3 (a compliant campaign end to end).

## Preflight
- Open b2, hit "Expand all", and confirm the consent diagrams render and zoom.
- Have one clearly-bad consent line and one clean line ready to contrast side by side.
- Be ready to explain the three deemed-consent routes without jargon.
- Have the s.16 withdrawal steps (inform, cease, record) to hand for the closing exercise.

## Never-cut beats
- **Valid consent needs a positive action and evidence.** A pre-ticked box plus a customer who never touched it is not consent - the person has to do the agreeing, and you have to be able to show it.
- **Deemed consent by notification (s.15A) is a real route but has conditions.** It needs a documented no-adverse-effect assessment plus notice and a reasonable opt-out window. A bought list or a short non-reply is not deemed consent.
- **Withdrawal cannot be blocked.** Under s.16 you may inform of consequences, but you must then cease use - PDPC rule of thumb about 10 business days - and record the withdrawal.

## Cuts if running long
Compress the "rewrite a bad consent line" build to a single shared example done together rather than individual work. Keep the three deemed-consent routes and the withdrawal rule - those are the exam-fail traps. Trim the s.15(3) contractual-necessity detail first.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Is an unticked opt-out box consent?"** No. Consent needs an affirmative action; failing to opt out is not agreeing.
- **"How fast must we act on a withdrawal?"** Cease use within a reasonable time - the PDPC rule of thumb is about 10 business days - and record it.
- **"Can we treat 'didn't reply to our notice' as deemed consent?"** Only via a proper s.15A route with an assessment and an opt-out window - not from silence alone.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines before acting. If DNC comes up, keep the two mistaught points straight: the register check is **21 days, not 30**, and the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers **voice too**. Within this session, the common error is treating pre-ticked boxes or silence as valid consent.
