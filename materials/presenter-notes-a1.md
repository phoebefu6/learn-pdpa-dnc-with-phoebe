# Presenter notes - Leader track session 1: Why PDPA + DNC matter

**Session goal (one line).** Give leaders the shape of both halves of the PDPA - the ten data-protection obligations and the DNC layer - and make the cost of getting it wrong real with actual PDPC penalties.

## Run of show (45 min)

Leader rhythm: 3 welcome / 17 concepts / 20 exercises / 5 Q&A. The two SVG diagrams carry Parts 1 and 2, so present at the projector, not the slides.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Set the frame: the PDPA is not the compliance team's problem alone, it is a leadership call about funding controls and asking the right questions. Read the "educational, not legal advice" banner out loud once - it appears on every page and you own it in the room.
- **3-12 Part 1 - Two halves of one law (9 min).** Walk the two-box diagram: data-protection obligations (all personal data) vs DNC provisions (marketing to SG phone numbers). Same law, two rulebooks. Land the line: most orgs remember the first half and forget the second until a marketing blast hits a listed number.
- **12-20 Part 2 - What it actually costs (8 min).** Two failure paths - a data leak (Protection Obligation) and unwanted marketing (DNC). Anchor with real figures: MBS S$315k, Ezynetic S$17.5k, the bought-list case S$16.8k. Do the self-study PDPC/penalty-cap card only if a leader asks.
- **20-32 Exercise 1 - Map your exposure (12 min).** Pen and paper. Three biggest data pools, red/amber/green on security, then the marketing channels with "do we check DNC, can we prove consent?" Circle the single reddest cell.
- **32-40 Exercise 2 - Price a breach (8 min).** Turn one pool into a real number: affected count, the 500+ notifiable threshold, direct plus indirect cost versus the cost of reasonable security.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Land on the five DPO/marketing questions in the homework as the concrete takeaway.

## Never-cut beats
- **Two separate tests.** DNC compliance and data-protection compliance are different tests - you can pass one and fail the other. This is exactly why the bought-list salesperson still got penalised despite checking DNC.
- **Reasonable security, not "were you attacked".** The PDPC penalises unreasonable arrangements, not the fact of an attack. The question "were your arrangements reasonable for the data you held?" is one leaders can fund an answer to before an incident.
- **The quiet cost.** The penalty is the headline; the customer trust you lose is the bill that keeps arriving. Say this out loud - it is what moves budgets.

## Quiz answers
1. **C** - The two rulebooks are the data-protection obligations (all personal data) and the Do Not Call provisions (marketing to SG phone numbers). Not government-vs-company, not local-vs-overseas.
2. **A** - DNC compliance and data-protection compliance are separate tests; checking DNC does not prove the people consented or were notified. In the real case the DNC check was mitigating, not exonerating.
3. **B** - The PDPC asks whether your security arrangements were reasonable for the data you held, not whether the attacker was sophisticated or whether you carried insurance.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Are we too small for the PDPA to care?"** No. Ezynetic was an SME and still drew a penalty plus directions. Size changes the number, not the obligation.
- **"Is checking DNC enough to run marketing safely?"** No - it clears the DNC test only. You still need a lawful basis (consent or proper notification) for holding and using the data. Both tests must pass.
- **"What is the penalty ceiling?"** For a data-protection breach, up to S$1M or 10% of Singapore turnover for larger orgs, whichever is higher (since 1 Oct 2022). The DNC caps come later in a5.

## Compliance caution
This session is educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines before acting. Even in this overview, foreground the two points people most often get wrong later: the DNC register check is valid for **21 days, not 30** (the stale brochure figure, corrected in a3/b1), and the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers **voice too**, not just text/fax (a4/b5).
