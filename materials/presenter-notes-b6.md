# Presenter notes - Practitioner track session 6: Access & correction

**Session goal (one line).** Turn access and correction requests into one calm, repeatable process - what to provide, the 30-day clock, and when you may refuse.

## Run of show (45 min)

Practitioner rhythm: 3 welcome / 15 concepts / 22 build-along / 5 Q&A. Part 1 runs to about the 22 mark and Part 2 to 40; the workflow build lives inside them.

- **0-3 Welcome.** Frame: these requests feel scary but run on a simple, repeatable process. Read the not-legal-advice banner.
- **3-22 Part 1 - The access right plus the 30-day clock (9 min plus discussion).** s.21: provide the data AND how it was used or disclosed in the past year. Respond as soon as reasonably possible; if not within 30 days, send a written notice within 30 days stating the date you will respond by.
- **22-40 Part 2 - Correction and refusals (9 min) plus build.** s.22: correct errors and pass corrections to third parties who received the data in the past year. When you may refuse, and what to do on rejection. Build-along: draft Marina's access-request workflow.
- **40-45 Q&A.** Preview b7 (breach response).

## Never-cut beats
- **Access has two halves.** You provide the data itself AND the usage story for the past year (how it was used and disclosed). Teams deliver the data and forget the year of use-and-disclosure info.
- **The 30-day clock does not just expire.** If you cannot respond within 30 days, you owe a written notice within 30 days stating the date you will respond by. Silence is a breach.
- **On rejection, preserve the data at least 30 days.** The individual's recourse is asking the PDPC to review your decision - the data must still exist for that.

## Quiz answers
1. **C** - An access request under s.21 requires you to provide the individual the data itself AND how it was used or disclosed in the past year. Teams often forget the second half.
2. **B** - If you cannot respond within 30 days, you must give a written notice within 30 days stating the date you will respond by. The clock does not simply expire.
3. **A** - On rejecting an access request you must preserve the relevant data for at least 30 days, because the individual's recourse is asking the PDPC to review, and the data must still exist.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- **"Do we just send them their data?"** Not quite - also the past-year story of how it was used and disclosed. Both halves.
- **"What if 30 days isn't enough?"** Respond as soon as reasonably possible, but send a written notice within 30 days giving the date you will respond by.
- **"We refused a request - can we delete the data?"** Not yet. Preserve it for at least 30 days so the person can ask the PDPC to review.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice - verify against current PDPC guidelines before acting. If DNC comes up, keep the two mistaught points straight: the register check is **21 days, not 30**, and the ongoing-relationship exclusion covers **voice too**. Within this session, note that multiple 30-day timelines exist (respond-or-notify, preserve-on-rejection) - keep them distinct, and do not confuse any with the DNC 21-day check.
