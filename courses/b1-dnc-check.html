The one habit that keeps you compliant

The Do Not Call rules look intimidating from the outside and turn out to be one disciplined habit on the inside: scope the message, then either hold consent or hold a fresh register check. Part 1 · covers the registers + the check duty

Three registers, one duty 7 min live

A Singapore number (8 digits starting 3, 6, 8, or 9) can be added to any of three registers. Before you send a marketing message on a channel, you must confirm the number is not on that channel's register - unless you have consent. This flow is the whole session.

LiveThe three registers + what a "specified message" is3 min▶

The registry has three registers: No Voice Call, No Text Message, No Fax Message. An individual can opt into any or all. When you submit numbers to check, they are checked against all three at once.

  • A "specified message" is the kind DNC governs: its purpose is to advertise, promote, or offer goods, services, land, or a business/investment opportunity (or to promote the supplier). In plain terms - marketing.
  • Not everything is a specified message. Transactional and service messages (a receipt, a delivery notice, a recall), messages to another organisation for its business (B2B), emergencies, market research, and messages within an ongoing relationship are all excluded. Session b5 works through these; for now, know that "is this even a specified message?" is always the first question.
  • Singapore numbers only: the DNC duty applies to Singapore telephone numbers - 8 digits beginning 3, 6, 8, or 9.
LiveThe 21-day rule - the detail that trips people3 min▶

Before sending, you must hold a confirmation that the number is not listed - and that confirmation is only valid for 21 days. Check on the 2nd, and it is good through the 23rd; after that you must re-check before sending again.

  • Watch the stale figure. Some older PDPC material still says "30 days". That was the rule before 1 February 2021. The current period is 21 days - teach and use 21.
  • Consent is the alternative, not an add-on. If you have clear, specific consent to market to that number, you do not need the register check at all - and consent lets you message even a listed number.
  • Buying a "DNC-cleaned" list does not transfer the duty. If you send, cause to send, or authorise sending, the obligation to hold a valid check is yours.
Real world

Marina Retail's near-miss. Marina's team pulled a list they had checked six weeks earlier for a "quick reminder blast". Six weeks is well past 21 days - the check had lapsed, and several numbers had been added to the register in between. Re-checking first turned a potential breach into a routine send. The habit is not "check once" - it is "check within 21 days, every time".

The two rulings people find surprising: a rewards offer to a current cardholder by voice call is out of scope (the ongoing-relationship exclusion applies to voice too, under the current Act), and a checked-clear SMS with no sender ID still fails - on s.44, not s.43. Passing the register check is necessary, not sufficient.

Self-studyHow the check actually happens (dnc.gov.sg)4 min read▶

Operationally, the register check runs through the PDPC's DNC Registry portal at dnc.gov.sg. The flow an organisation follows:

Set up an organisation account on the DNC Registry portal (and optional sub-accounts for teams or campaigns).

Submit the Singapore numbers you intend to market to. The registry returns which are listed on which register.

Record the date and result. Your "not listed" confirmation is valid for 21 days - note the expiry against the campaign.

Send only to the numbers that came back clear (or to which you hold consent), within the 21-day window.

One number to confirm yourself The registry charges fees for checking (and there are free allowances), but the exact figures live inside the logged-in portal and change over time - so this course does not quote a dollar amount. Check the current fee schedule in your dnc.gov.sg account before budgeting a large campaign.

On consent withdrawal, you must stop marketing to that number and re-check the register before any further sends - the prescribed period to give effect to a withdrawal is also 21 days.

Check yourself

Three questions before you go 🎯 ◐ 90 seconds

1 · A "not listed" confirmation from the DNC Registry is valid for...

The current validity is 21 days. The "30 days" you may still see in older PDPC material is the pre-2021 rule - stale. Re-check within 21 days before every send.

2 · You have clear, specific consent to market to a number that IS on the register. You may...

Consent is an alternative lawful basis to the register check, and it overrides listing. That is why the checker treats "consent on file" as a pass even when the number is listed.

3 · Your number came back clear on the register, but the SMS has no sender identity. The send is...

Passing the register check is necessary but not sufficient. Every specified message must identify the sender and how to contact them (s.44) - a separate duty from the check.
